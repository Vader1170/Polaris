01. Audience

Who is Polaris for?

It is built specifically for curious 14-to-18-year-old students who want to explore deep questions and ambitious projects on their own. Whether you are preparing for a science competition, a humanity monograph, an engineering build, or simply wondering how real scholarly investigation operates, Polaris serves as a reliable guide to help you find your bearings.

02. How It Works

How Polaris Works

Polaris is an interactive tool that turns a student's interests and constraints into a step-by-step research or project plan.

It works through ten short question-navigators, each built for a different kind of work:

  1. Research
  2. Science Fair
  3. Olympiad
  4. Portfolio
  5. Debate
  6. Project
  7. Learning
  8. Paper Review
  9. Journal
  10. Career

After you answer the questions, an AI generates a roadmap built around what you actually typed in — a reading list, a weekly schedule, tools to use, common mistakes to avoid, and your next three actions.

One thing it doesn't do: it won't write the work for you. It plans the path — you still walk it.
